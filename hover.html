Overview

A few classmates and I decided to build a leafblower-powered hovercraft. Made of a plywood base with a hole in it to insert the leafblower, the hovercraft worked by blowing air under the base and through a holey plastic tarp taped underneath. The tarp would expand with the air blown into it, and the holes in the tarp would let it escape in a controlled way and lift the craft off the floor. This made a little cusion of air between the craft and the floor, eliminating friction and allowing the craft to float around. A lot like an air hockey puck! Mark II would probably benefit from a cordless leafblower.
