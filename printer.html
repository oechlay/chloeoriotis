Overview

I designed a functioning kit-style 3D printer in my second-year Mechanical Engineering Design class. My team and I chose to design a delta 3-arm 3D printer was because it provides a speedy print with minimal vibrations. Its parts and moveable assembly were modelled in SolidWorks.

Skills Used

SolidWorks was the star of the show in this project, but I also drew on my mechanism design experience from the course.

What I Learned

Converting constant rotational motion from the motors into the Cartesian output motion of the print head was difficult. This was overcome by using lead screws along the height of the printer (converting rotational to linear motion) and connecting three sets of parallel linkages from the lead screw nuts to ball joints on the print head (converting linear to Cartesian motion). This was also my first experience with CAD software!
