2019

Overview

For this in-class consulting project, a fictional client wanted to know which shower alternative they should implement in their environmentally conscious hospital: a shower with a bathtub bottom or a shower enclosure with a door.

Skills Used

My team and I focused the analysis on the bathtub and the door, made of acrylic and glass respectively, and used a function, objectives, and constraints analysis and a streamlined life cycle assessment analysis as a preliminary investigation. An economic analysis, a quantitative economic input-output life cycle analysis and a societal analysis helped the team solidify a recommendation. A sensitivity analysis aided in determining the better alternative by considering the effects of assumptions.

What I Learned

Designing For the Environment is an important task for engineers, as we have an obligation to protect the environment and the general public that lives (and will live) in it.
