2017, 2018

Overview

I participated in the 2017 and 2018 University of Toronto Engineering Competitions (UTEK), winning first place both years and having the opportunity to compete in the Ontario Engineering Competitions held in Ottawa.

In 2017 my team and I won in the Junior Design Competition, where we designed a device to passively sort various sediments into certain jars as they passed by on a conveyor belt. Our design included four elevated reservoirs with holes at the bottom, covered by sliding doors. The doors had a uniquely cut tab on them (the "lock"), that would only be triggered by the correct jar (with the matching "key") that sild under the machine on the conveyor belt. As the door opened, the sediment would spill into the correct jar. The 2017 Ontario Engineering Competition had our team design and build a battery-powered car that would traverse a curving path of varying elevations.

In 2018, our team came together again to win UTEK's Re-Engineering Competition, where we designed a conceptual system for transporting mined Martian ice to a centralized location on Mars. The 2018 OEC Re-Engineering Competition had us design a system for public transit on a Mars colony.

I participated in these competitions when I was in my first and second years of school, and sometimes I like just looking back at my designs and appreciating how far I've come. Noticing all the things that could be improved upon is proof to me that I've developed a critical eye and gained a lot of technical knowledge in my undergrad.
