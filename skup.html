2017-2018

Overview

I was elected by the student body to be the Project Director of Skule Kup in the 2017-2018 academic year. Skule Kup is a year-long battle of the engineering disciplines, where all students are encouraged to socialize and make new friends as they compete in friendly games and challenges around campus. My responsibilities included hiring and overseeing an executive team, planning and running 9 months of activities, organizing the year's budget, running multiple Skule Kup social media platforms, and finally interviewing and transitioning the next director. This was my first experience being a member of the Engineering Society, and although it was a daunting task for a student just finishing her first year, I'm glad I went for it. Being a Director allowed me to improve my interpersonal, organizational and time management skills.

Leading an executive team comes with its challenges, including lack of interest as the year went on, midterms and exams taking time away from the exec team, and intrateam conflict. I tried my best to resolve the issues, however, I realized I lacked the experience needed to make this work. I reached out for help from previous directors and eventually overcame the issues my team faced, leading to a very successful year! I'm most proud of my work with Ryerson University's Engineering Society and organizing an interschool glow-in-the-dark capture the flag competition. It received the highest student turnout of all the Skule Kup events, and was a lot of fun to play as well!
