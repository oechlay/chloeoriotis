Overview

U of T was a training ground and makeshift camp for soldiers enlisted in the First World War. In the Second World War, the Engineering faculty was transported to the Ajax Campus, where students and veterens were taught applied science. Many soldiers were brought to U of T to learn specialized engineering sills before going to Europe, and fellow Skule students halted their studies to join the fight.

On Remembrance Day in 2018, we honoured their bravery with a monument erected on Front Campus. I designed corrugaded sides that displayed a painting of a soldier in a field or "Lest We Forget" and "Engineers Remember the Wars", depending on the angle they were viewed at. The smaller sides of the monument gave information on U of T's role in helping win the Wars, which was accompanied by a recorded narration.
