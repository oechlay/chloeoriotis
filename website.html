2020

Overview

I coded this site from scratch! This project began when I realized my old portfolio desperately needed a revamp, and to me that meant something a little more exciting than a powerpoint or pdf. I wanted something that would showcase my technical skills, but also my personality. I saw this as an opportunity to learn a new skill, and I knew this would be a great time to try web development.

I believe in lifelong learning and I wanted to prove to myself I could learn an entirely new concept (web dev) on my own, outside of school. I sought online resources that would give me the fundamentals of HTML and CSS, and developed the site while completing my fourth year of school.

This site's always going to be a work in progress. As much as I try to reach perfection, I know there will always be something that could be updated, improved, or even overhauled. That's a journey I love taking!

Skills Used

I used HTML to write the structure of the site and its pages, CSS to style everything and make the content pleasant to read, and a little JavaScript to make the lightboxes happen. I used GitHub to publish the site online.

I'd never applied the engineering design process to a coding-only project on such a huge scale. Problem solving is much more difficult when the thing that's not working can't be handled or manipulated easily. That's where Chrome's developer tools shined! I also made the background art using some of my favourite colours.

What I Learned

Responsive coding (making sites for mobile as well as desktop) is something that needs to be thought about early on! Making sure content is readable on screens of varying aspect ratios and sizes was one of the most challenging aspects of this project. Lesson learned: code for mobile to begin, then modify for larger screens.

Other than the new coding languages and GitHub, I showed myself I could learn a new skill without the structure of school. It's given me confidence that my perseverence and dedication to doing things to high standards will stick with me after my undergrad. I'm ready to tackle the next big project I set my mind to!
